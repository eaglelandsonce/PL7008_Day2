DEAL SUMMARY - PROJECT LIGHTHOUSE (FICTIONAL TRAINING DATA)
Status: Executed - December 2024
Transaction type: Pensioner buy-in, GBP 340m premium
Ceding insurer: Crownfield Insurance Company (UK)
Scheme profile: UK engineering sector, 2,200 members, 70% pensioner liabilities
Benefit structure: LPI 0/5 increases, 50% spouse's pension, GMP for pre-1997 service

Outcome: Won at second-round pricing. Executed within 5 months of first quote.

Key lessons and sticking points:
1. Crownfield insisted on a broad data warranty. Legal negotiation on the warranty
   consumed 6 weeks. Recommend raising warranty scope in round one term sheets.
2. Spouse data was materially incomplete (12% of married members missing spouse DOB).
   We priced with a conservative spouse-age assumption (male 3 years older) and
   included a data true-up mechanism at 18 months. True-up worked well.
3. GMP equalisation adjustments were not finalised at execution; a holdback of 1.5%
   of premium was agreed.
4. CMI projection basis debate: trustees' advisers used a higher long-term improvement
   rate (1.5%) than our house view (1.25%). Bridging analysis was decisive in winning.

Counterparty notes: Crownfield commercial team responsive but legal team slow.
Trustee (independent chair) focused heavily on collateral structure.
